B. J. Habibie when he served as president of Indonesia

Here's a time line of B. J. Habibie's life:

  • 1936 - Habibie was born in Parepare, South Sulawesi, Indonesia
  • 1954 - He studied at the Bandung Institute of Technology (ITB) majoring in mechanical engineering
  • 1955 - Habibie continued his aeronautical engineering studies, specializing in aircraft construction, at RWTH Aachen, West Germany with a scholarship from the Indonesian government
  • 1960 - Habibie earned a Diploma in Engineering with a cum laude predicate, and five years later he earned a Doctor of Engineering degree also with a cum laude predicate
  • 1962 - After returning from his studies in Germany, Habibie then married Hasri Ainun Besari (Ainun), a high school friend
  • 1973 - Habibie is believed to be the first leader of Nusantara Aircraft Industry (IPTN)
  • 1978 - After being trusted as the leader of IPTN, Habibie was then appointed as State Minister of Research and Technology
  • 1995 - With passion and persistence, Habibie and his team at IPTN successfully tested the first commercial aircraft made in Indonesia, namely the IPTN N-250
  • 1998 - On March 11, 1998, Habibie officially became Vice President of Indonesia. However, this position did not last long, in the 1998 crisis Habibie then rose to the position of president to replace Suharto who had stepped down
  • 2019 - The third president of Indonesia, Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie or familiarly known as B. J. Habibie died on Wednesday, September 11, 2019 at the age of 83
